1. Trust erosion takes time to build and can collapse quickly due to various scandals or failures.
2. Rebuilding trust requires consistent and transparent communication which is challenging to maintain over time.
3. Previous negative experiences can create deep-seated skepticism that is hard to overcome.
4. The impact of misinformation and fake news complicates efforts to restore public confidence.
5. Trust in institutions often depends on perceived competence and integrity, both of which may have been compromised.
6. Rapid changes or inconsistent policies can undermine efforts to regain public trust.
7. Economic downturns or social crises can exacerbate doubts about an organization's ability to effectively address issues.
8. Historical patterns of failure can lead to a self-fulfilling prophecy where people expect further disappointments.
